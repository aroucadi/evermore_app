# Recall Persona Canvas

---

## Primary Persona: Margaret (Senior User)

### Demographics

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Age | 78 |
| Location | Suburban Ohio, USA |
| Living situation | Widowed, lives alone |
| Education | High school, some college |
| Former occupation | Elementary school teacher |
| Income | Fixed (Social Security, modest savings) |

### Technology Profile

| Dimension | Level |
|-----------|-------|
| Smartphone usage | Basic (calls, photos) |
| App comfort | Very low |
| Voice assistant experience | Minimal (occasional Alexa) |
| Typing ability | Slow, arthritis-affected |
| Screen reading | Requires large text, good contrast |

### Goals

1. Preserve life stories for grandchildren who live far away
2. Have someone to talk to on quiet afternoons
3. Remember details about family history before they fade
4. Feel useful and valued despite aging

### Frustrations

1. Technology that requires typing, passwords, or small buttons
2. Family too busy to call regularly
3. Forgetting names and dates that used to come easily
4. Being dismissed as "just an old lady"

### Motivations

| Motivation | Quote |
|------------|-------|
| Legacy | "I want my grandchildren to know where they came from." |
| Connection | "I just want someone to talk to." |
| Memory | "If I say it out loud, maybe I won't forget." |
| Purpose | "I still have things to contribute." |

### A Day in Margaret's Life

6:30 AM - Wakes early, makes coffee, watches morning news  
9:00 AM - Waters plants, looks at family photos  
11:00 AM - Senior center or errands (2-3 days/week)  
1:00 PM - Lunch alone, quiet afternoon  
3:00 PM - **Potential Recall session time**  
5:00 PM - Light dinner, TV  
7:00 PM - Reads, calls family if they're available  
9:00 PM - Bed  

### Relationship to Recall

| Dimension | Expectation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Initiation | Recall should prompt her, not require her to "figure out" how to start |
| Pace | Slow, patient, never rushed |
| Memory | Recalls past conversations ("You told me about your sister last week...") |
| Empathy | Responds to emotions, not just facts |
| Output | Knows her stories are being saved for family |

## Secondary Persona: Sarah (Family Member)

### Demographics

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Age | 47 |
| Location | Seattle, WA (500 miles from mother) |
| Living situation | Married, two teenagers |
| Occupation | Marketing director |
| Income | Upper middle class |

### Technology Profile

| Dimension | Level |
|-----------|-------|
| General tech comfort | High |
| App usage | Daily, multiple platforms |
| Smart home devices | Several |
| Willingness to pay for solutions | High for parent care |

### Goals

1. Stay connected to mother despite distance and busy schedule
2. Capture mother's stories before memory declines further
3. Give children connection to their grandmother's history
4. Reduce guilt about not being present more often

### Frustrations

1. Phone calls feel rushed and repetitive
2. Mother repeats stories but Sarah misses the full context
3. No time for dedicated "oral history" project
4. Worry about mother being isolated and lonely
5. Distance makes casual check-ins difficult

### Motivations

| Motivation | Quote |
|------------|-------|
| Legacy | "I wish I had recorded my dad's stories before he passed." |
| Guilt relief | "At least I know she's talking to someone." |
| Connection | "I want to hear her stories in her own voice." |
| Heritage | "My kids need to know their roots." |

### Relationship to Recall

| Dimension | Expectation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Dashboard | Weekly view of mother's sessions and stories |
| Alerts | Notifications if anything concerning arises |
| Contributions | Ability to send photos or prompts to trigger conversations |
| Control | Manage subscription, settings, sharing permissions |
| Output | Download stories, create printed books (future) |
