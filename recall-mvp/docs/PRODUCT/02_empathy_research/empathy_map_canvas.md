# Recall Empathy Map Canvas

> Empathy map for Margaret, our primary senior persona.

---

## User Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Name | Margaret |
| Age | 78 |
| Living situation | Independent, suburban home |
| Technology comfort | Low; uses phone for calls, struggles with apps |
| Cognitive status | Mild memory changes, fully lucid |
| Social context | Widowed, children live 500+ miles away |

---

## Says

Direct quotes captured from user research:

> "I have so many stories, but no one has time to listen."

> "My grandchildren don't know anything about their great-grandparents."

> "I forget things now. Sometimes I can't remember if I told someone a story already."

> "I don't want to bother my kids. They're so busy."

> "I wish I had asked my mother more questions before she passed."

> "The phone is fine, but those apps confuse me."

---

## Thinks

Inferences from observation and reading between the lines:

- "My stories matter, but maybe I'm being selfish wanting to share them."
- "Technology is for young people. I'll never learn it."
- "I don't want to be a burden to my family."
- "What happens to everything I remember when I'm gone?"
- "I used to be so sharp. This forgetfulness is embarrassing."
- "Will anyone care about my life after I'm gone?"

---

## Feels

| Emotion | Context |
|---------|---------|
| **Loneliness** | Days pass without meaningful conversation |
| **Nostalgia** | Rich memories triggered by photos, music, places |
| **Anxiety** | Fear of forgetting important things |
| **Pride** | Life accomplishments, family history |
| **Frustration** | Technology that doesn't work for her |
| **Invisibility** | Feeling overlooked or dismissed due to age |
| **Hope** | That her stories will live on |

---

## Does

Observable behaviors:

- Talks to family photos when alone
- Keeps photo albums organized but rarely shares them
- Attends senior center programs for social contact
- Avoids technology that requires typing or complex navigation
- Repeats stories to anyone who will listen
- Writes occasional notes to remember things

---

## Pains

| Pain | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Memory gaps** | Frustration when she cannot recall a name or detail |
| **Technology friction** | Apps that require passwords, typing, or visual navigation exclude her |
| **Loneliness** | Long stretches without meaningful human interaction |
| **Fear of exploitation** | Awareness of scams targeting seniors |
| **Mortality anxiety** | Worry that her stories will die with her |
| **Family guilt** | Not wanting to burden busy children |

---

## Gains

| Gain | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Feeling heard** | Someone listens patiently and asks follow-up questions |
| **Memory reinforcement** | Talking about the past helps her remember |
| **Legacy creation** | Knowing her stories will live on for grandchildren |
| **Daily purpose** | A reason to engage, share, and contribute |
| **Family connection** | Knowing her children receive and value her stories |
| **Dignity** | Being treated as a person with valuable experiences |

---

## Implications for Recall

| Insight | Design Implication |
|---------|-------------------|
| Says "no one has time" | Recall must be always available, never rushed |
| Thinks she's a burden | Proactive engagement removes need to "ask" for attention |
| Feels nostalgic with photos | Photo-triggered conversations leverage natural memory cues |
| Does avoid typing | Voice-first is mandatory, not optional |
| Pains around exploitation | Safety guardrails must be architecturally embedded |
| Gains from legacy | Family dashboard shows tangible output from sessions |
